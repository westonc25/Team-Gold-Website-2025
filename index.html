Overview

Smart City uses technology, data, and intelligent systems to make urban life more efficient,
sustainable, and connected to help mitigate rapid urbanization. By integrating real-time data
from user and external APIs, we can reduce traffic congestion, improve public safety, and
enhance quality of life for the future of creating cities that work for the people,
not just around them.

Urban Overcrowding Problem

Rapid urbanization and inadequate planning have caused severe overcrowding in cities, significantly diminishing residents' quality of life. Mass migration to urban areas, coupled with data gaps that obstruct effective urban planning, has led to strained infrastructure causing health issues, overburdened road systems resulting in traffic congestion, poor quality housing that fails to meet demand, inadequate waste management systems, water scarcity impacting daily needs, and rising crime rates threatening safety. These challenges contribute to longer commutes, unaffordable housing, and a decline in community well-being, highlighting the critical need for enhanced urban planning and resource management to address this growing crisis.
